Case Connectives

Basic sentences can be constructed from a single leaf to express that it is contextually significant.

Reén

reén

rain

It is/was/will rain.

However, most sentences require more complex structures. The case connectives, i, lí, jií, and gen, are all particles that assign a case to their first argument in relation to their second argument. i attaches a clause with the agentive case to another clause, lí attaches a clause with the patientive case, jií with the dative case, and gen with the genitive case. These can also be stacked together to attach any combination of an agent, a patient, a beneficiary, and possessor to a clause. Case connectives are applied in order from most contextually significant to least.
